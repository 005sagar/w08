<h1> <b> Sphero </b> </h1>
<li>
The Sphero is one of these tools. It is amazingly versatile and will engage and empower your students. </li> <br>
<li>A tennis ball sized robot connected via bluetooth to a mobile device, the Sphero can be used to transform teaching and learning across various curriculum areas.
</li><br>
<li>
Sphero was initially prototyped by its inventors, Ian Bernstein and Adam Wilson, with a 3D-printed shell and electronics taken from a smartphone.
</li><br>
<li>
They are controlled with a smartphone or tablet running iOS, Android or Windows Phone via Bluetooth, and wirelessly charged with a charging base.
</li><br>
<li>
Since they have an accelerometer and a gyroscope, it can also be used as a controller for games on iOS and Android platforms.
</li><br>
<li>
Sphero's firmware is updated automatically with the official app.</li><br>
<li>
An SDK is also available, making it possible to develop applications that can interact with the ball. </li> <br>
<li>
Unofficial SDKs are also available for other devices and platforms, such as Robot Operating System.
</li>
